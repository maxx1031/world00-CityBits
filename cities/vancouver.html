地理 & 城市结构Geography & Urban Structure
001

温哥华面积仅115平方公里，但大温哥华地区（Metro Vancouver）面积超过2800平方公里，人口约270万。

Vancouver covers just 115 square kilometers, but Metro Vancouver spans over 2,800 sq km with a population of roughly 2.7 million.

002

温哥华北靠北岸山脉，西临太平洋，三面环水一面靠山的地形使其成为全球最具自然景观的城市之一。

Vancouver is backed by the North Shore Mountains to the north and the Pacific Ocean to the west. Its water-on-three-sides, mountain-on-one geography makes it one of the most scenically stunning cities in the world.

003

Stanley Park（斯坦利公园）占地405公顷，几乎与温哥华市中心的面积相当，是北美最大的城市公园之一。

Stanley Park covers 405 hectares — roughly the same size as downtown Vancouver — making it one of the largest urban parks in North America.

004

温哥华的市中心半岛三面环水——北面是Burrard Inlet，南面是False Creek，西面是English Bay，只有东面与大陆相连。

Downtown Vancouver is a peninsula surrounded by water on three sides — Burrard Inlet to the north, False Creek to the south, and English Bay to the west, with only the east connecting to the mainland.

005

温哥华的城市规划在全球独树一帜——它通过"塔楼+裙楼"模式（Vancouverism）在市中心建设了大量细长的住宅高楼，底部是商业和公共空间。这种规划既保证了高密度居住，又最大限度地保留了每栋楼的景观视野和街道尺度。这一模式被城市规划界称为"温哥华主义"，被迪拜、墨尔本等城市模仿。

Vancouver's urban planning is globally distinctive — through its "tower-and-podium" model (Vancouverism), the city built numerous slender residential towers downtown, with commercial and public spaces at the base. This design ensures high-density living while maximizing views and maintaining street-level scale. Known as "Vancouverism" in planning circles, it has been emulated by cities like Dubai and Melbourne.

006

温哥华是加拿大唯一一座没有高速公路穿城的大城市——1960年代市民成功阻止了一条计划穿过唐人街的高速公路。

Vancouver is the only major Canadian city with no freeway running through it — in the 1960s, citizens successfully blocked a planned highway that would have cut through Chinatown.

007

Granville Island原本是一个工业区，1970年代改造为公共市场和艺术区，是城市更新的经典案例。

Granville Island was originally an industrial zone. Transformed in the 1970s into a public market and arts district, it is a classic case of urban renewal.

008

温哥华的Gastown是城市发源地，以"Gassy" Jack Deighton命名——他于1867年在此开了第一家酒吧。

Vancouver's Gastown is the city's birthplace, named after "Gassy" Jack Deighton, who opened the first saloon here in 1867.

「温哥华是一座你分不清山和城市边界的地方，
因为大自然就是城市的一部分。」

"In Vancouver you can't tell where the mountains end
and the city begins — nature is part of the city."

地铁 & 交通Transit & Transport
009

温哥华的SkyTrain是世界上最长的全自动无人驾驶轻轨系统之一，全线无司机，运营超过80公里。

Vancouver's SkyTrain is one of the longest fully automated, driverless light rail systems in the world, operating over 80 km of track without drivers.

010

SkyTrain于1985年为世博会（Expo 86）而建，最初只有一条线路，如今已扩展为三条线路。

SkyTrain opened in 1985 for Expo 86. It started with just one line and has since expanded to three.

011

温哥华的SeaBus是一种渡轮公交，横跨Burrard Inlet连接市中心与北温哥华，航程仅12分钟。

Vancouver's SeaBus is a ferry transit service crossing Burrard Inlet to connect downtown with North Vancouver in just 12 minutes.

012

温哥华是北美自行车通勤率最高的城市之一，拥有超过300公里的自行车道网络。城市规划者将自行车基础设施视为优先事项，Hornby Street和Dunsmuir Street的分离式自行车道是北美城市规划的标杆。即使在多雨的冬天，也有大量市民骑车通勤——温哥华人对雨的态度是"我们不用雨伞"。

Vancouver has one of the highest bicycle commuting rates in North America, with over 300 km of bike lanes. City planners prioritize cycling infrastructure — the separated bike lanes on Hornby and Dunsmuir streets are benchmarks for North American urban planning. Even in the rainy winters, many residents cycle to work — Vancouverites' attitude toward rain is "we don't use umbrellas."

013

温哥华国际机场（YVR）连续多年被评为北美最佳机场，航站楼内展示着大量原住民艺术品。

Vancouver International Airport (YVR) has been voted Best Airport in North America for many consecutive years. The terminal showcases extensive Indigenous artwork.

014

温哥华到惠斯勒的Sea-to-Sky Highway全长约120公里，沿途从海岸线攀升至山区，是北美最美的公路之一。

The Sea-to-Sky Highway from Vancouver to Whistler stretches about 120 km, climbing from coastline to mountains — one of North America's most scenic drives.

015

温哥华的Aquabus是一种彩色小型渡轮，在False Creek上穿梭，连接Granville Island和市中心。

Vancouver's Aquabus is a colorful small ferry that shuttles across False Creek, connecting Granville Island with downtown.

016

温哥华到西雅图的跨境巴士全程约4小时，很多温哥华人周末去西雅图购物，因为美国物价更低。

The cross-border bus from Vancouver to Seattle takes about 4 hours. Many Vancouverites make weekend shopping trips to Seattle, where US prices are lower.

食物 & 饮食文化Food & Dining Culture
017

温哥华被称为"北美寿司之都"，寿司店密度和品质在北美首屈一指，大量日本移民带来了正宗的寿司文化。

Vancouver is called the "sushi capital of North America" — sushi shop density and quality are unrivaled on the continent, thanks to a large Japanese immigrant community that brought authentic sushi culture.

018

温哥华发明了BC卷（BC Roll）——用烤三文鱼皮代替传统的鱼肉做成的寿司卷，是温哥华对世界寿司文化的独特贡献。

Vancouver invented the BC Roll — a sushi roll made with grilled salmon skin instead of traditional fish. It's Vancouver's unique contribution to world sushi culture.

019

温哥华的中餐水平是北美最高的，Richmond（列治文）被誉为"北美最好的中餐城市"，广式点心和川菜水准直逼大陆一线城市。

Vancouver's Chinese food is the best in North America. Richmond is hailed as "the best Chinese food city in North America" — its Cantonese dim sum and Sichuan cuisine rival restaurants in mainland China's top cities.

020

温哥华的亚洲美食之丰富在整个西方世界中几乎无出其右——从正宗的广式早茶到日本居酒屋，从韩国烤肉到越南河粉，从印度咖喱到马来西亚叻沙，几乎亚洲每个国家的料理都能在温哥华找到极高水准的版本。这归功于温哥华超过50%的少数族裔人口，其中亚裔是最大群体。

Vancouver's Asian cuisine diversity is virtually unmatched in the Western world — from authentic Cantonese morning tea to Japanese izakayas, Korean BBQ to Vietnamese pho, Indian curry to Malaysian laksa, nearly every Asian country's cuisine can be found at an exceptionally high level. This is thanks to Vancouver's 50%+ visible minority population, with Asian residents being the largest group.

021

Granville Island Public Market是温哥华最著名的公共市场，汇集了新鲜海产、手工面包、本地奶酪和各种小吃。

Granville Island Public Market is Vancouver's most famous public market, featuring fresh seafood, artisan bread, local cheese, and an array of snacks.

022

温哥华的Japadog（日式热狗）是街头美食传奇——把照烧酱、海苔和日式蛋黄酱加在热狗上，风靡全城。

Vancouver's Japadog is a street food legend — a hot dog topped with teriyaki sauce, seaweed, and Japanese mayo that has taken the city by storm.

023

BC省的三文鱼种类繁多（五种太平洋三文鱼），每年秋天三文鱼洄游季节是温哥华最重要的自然和美食事件。

BC's salmon comes in five Pacific species. The annual autumn salmon run is Vancouver's most important natural and culinary event.

024

温哥华的精品咖啡文化极为发达，49th Parallel、Revolver Coffee等本地品牌在全加拿大享有盛誉。

Vancouver's specialty coffee scene is thriving. Local brands like 49th Parallel and Revolver Coffee are celebrated across Canada.

历史 & 政治History & Politics
025

温哥华建立在Musqueam、Squamish和Tsleil-Waututh三个原住民族群的传统领地上，他们在此居住了数千年。

Vancouver is built on the traditional territories of the Musqueam, Squamish, and Tsleil-Waututh First Nations, who have lived here for thousands of years.

026

温哥华城市的历史始于1867年Gassy Jack的酒吧，1886年正式建市，同年一场大火几乎烧毁了整座城市。

Vancouver's urban history began with Gassy Jack's saloon in 1867. The city was officially incorporated in 1886, the same year a massive fire nearly destroyed the entire settlement.

027

加拿大太平洋铁路（CPR）于1887年通车至温哥华，使其从一个小镇迅速发展为太平洋沿岸的重要港口城市。

The Canadian Pacific Railway (CPR) reached Vancouver in 1887, transforming it from a small town into a major Pacific port city almost overnight.

028

温哥华的华人移民史充满了血泪——19世纪末大量华工参与修建太平洋铁路，死亡率极高。1923年加拿大颁布《排华法案》禁止华人入境，直到1947年才废除。如今温哥华的华裔人口超过50万，约占大温地区的20%，唐人街是北美最古老的华埠之一，但也面临着士绅化的威胁。

Vancouver's Chinese immigration history is steeped in hardship — in the late 19th century, masses of Chinese laborers helped build the Pacific Railway, suffering extremely high mortality rates. In 1923, Canada passed the Chinese Exclusion Act, banning Chinese immigration until its repeal in 1947. Today, Vancouver's Chinese population exceeds 500,000, roughly 20% of Metro Vancouver. Chinatown is one of the oldest in North America but faces gentrification threats.

029

Expo 86（1986年世博会）是温哥华现代化的转折点，博览会用地后来发展成为今天繁华的Yaletown社区。

Expo 86 (the 1986 World Exposition) was a turning point in Vancouver's modernization. The former expo grounds later developed into today's thriving Yaletown neighborhood.

030

温哥华于2010年成功举办了冬季奥运会，借此机会大幅改善了城市基础设施和国际知名度。

Vancouver successfully hosted the 2010 Winter Olympics, using the occasion to significantly upgrade city infrastructure and boost international recognition.

031

温哥华以多元文化主义著称，超过50%的居民母语不是英语，城市拥有全加拿大最高的少数族裔比例。

Vancouver is renowned for multiculturalism — over 50% of residents speak a mother tongue other than English, giving the city the highest visible minority percentage in all of Canada.

032

温哥华的大麻合法化走在加拿大前列，2018年加拿大全国合法化之前，温哥华已经对大麻店采取了相对宽容的态度。

Vancouver's cannabis legalization was ahead of the curve. Before Canada's nationwide legalization in 2018, Vancouver had already adopted a relatively tolerant approach to cannabis shops.

建筑 & 空间Architecture & Space
033

Canada Place是温哥华的标志性建筑，白色帆形屋顶模仿了帆船的造型，是1986年世博会的加拿大馆。

Canada Place is Vancouver's landmark building. Its white sail-shaped roof mimics a sailing ship and was the Canada Pavilion for Expo 86.

034

温哥华公共图书馆中央馆由Moshe Safdie设计，外形酷似罗马斗兽场，是城市最受欢迎的公共建筑之一。

The Vancouver Public Library central branch, designed by Moshe Safdie, resembles the Roman Colosseum and is one of the city's most beloved public buildings.

035

Gastown的蒸汽钟建于1977年，是世界上少数仍在运行的蒸汽动力时钟之一，每15分钟喷出蒸汽并演奏音乐。

Gastown's Steam Clock, built in 1977, is one of the few still-functioning steam-powered clocks in the world, whistling and playing music every 15 minutes.

036

温哥华的玻璃高楼群是城市天际线的标志——为了最大化山景和海景，大部分住宅高楼都采用全玻璃幕墙设计。这些细长的玻璃塔在阳光下闪闪发光，在阴雨天则与灰色的天空融为一体。批评者认为这些建筑缺乏个性，支持者则赞赏它们与自然景观的和谐关系。

Vancouver's glass tower skyline is the city's trademark — to maximize mountain and ocean views, most residential towers use full glass curtain walls. These slender glass towers shimmer in sunlight and blend with the grey sky on rainy days. Critics say they lack character; supporters praise their harmony with the natural landscape.

037

Lions Gate Bridge建于1938年，横跨Burrard Inlet，其设计灵感来自金门大桥，是进入北温哥华和惠斯勒的必经之路。

Lions Gate Bridge, built in 1938, spans Burrard Inlet. Inspired by the Golden Gate Bridge, it's the gateway to North Vancouver and Whistler.

038

温哥华的Robson Square由著名建筑师Arthur Erickson设计，用屋顶花园覆盖了一整条街道，是城市公共空间的杰作。

Vancouver's Robson Square, designed by renowned architect Arthur Erickson, covers an entire street block with rooftop gardens — a masterpiece of public space design.

039

Science World的巨大银色球形建筑原本是Expo 86的展馆，如今是温哥华最受儿童欢迎的科学博物馆。

Science World's giant silver geodesic dome was originally an Expo 86 pavilion and is now Vancouver's most popular children's science museum.

040

温哥华的唐人街千禧门建于2002年，是北美最大的中式牌坊之一，标志着温哥华对华人社区的认可。

Vancouver's Chinatown Millennium Gate, built in 2002, is one of the largest Chinese-style archways in North America, symbolizing Vancouver's recognition of its Chinese community.

文化 & 社会Culture & Society
041

温哥华被称为"北方好莱坞"（Hollywood North），是北美第三大影视制作中心，大量美剧和电影在此取景。

Vancouver is known as "Hollywood North" — the third-largest film and TV production center in North America, where countless American shows and movies are shot.

042

《X档案》《超自然》《死侍》《五十度灰》等大量影视作品都在温哥华拍摄，街头经常可以遇到剧组在取景。

"The X-Files," "Supernatural," "Deadpool," and "Fifty Shades of Grey" were all filmed in Vancouver. Film crews are a common sight on the streets.

043

温哥华的原住民艺术极具特色，图腾柱、面具和版画是西北海岸原住民文化的代表，Stanley Park内有大量图腾柱展示。

Vancouver's Indigenous art is distinctive — totem poles, masks, and prints represent Northwest Coast Indigenous culture. Stanley Park features numerous totem pole displays.

044

温哥华的多元文化深度在全球城市中罕见——这里有超过200个民族的居民，使用超过150种语言。Richmond的街道上中文招牌比英文还多，旁遮普语在Surrey区随处可闻，Commercial Drive是意大利文化的据点，Main Street则是新潮文化的前沿。温哥华人的日常就是在不同文化之间自由切换。

Vancouver's multiculturalism runs remarkably deep — over 200 ethnic groups and 150+ languages coexist here. In Richmond, Chinese signage outnumbers English. Punjabi fills the air in Surrey. Commercial Drive is an Italian cultural hub, and Main Street is the cutting edge of indie culture. Daily life in Vancouver means seamlessly switching between cultures.

045

温哥华的户外运动文化极其浓厚，居民可以早上在北岸山脉滑雪，下午到English Bay冲浪，这在全球大城市中极为罕见。

Vancouver's outdoor sports culture is intense. Residents can ski on the North Shore Mountains in the morning and surf at English Bay in the afternoon — a rarity among major world cities.

046

温哥华人穿衣风格偏向休闲户外——Lululemon（总部在温哥华）瑜伽裤和Arc'teryx冲锋衣是本地人的"制服"。

Vancouverites dress in a casual outdoor style — Lululemon (headquartered in Vancouver) yoga pants and Arc'teryx shell jackets are the local "uniform."

047

Lululemon于1998年在温哥华Kitsilano社区创立，从一家小瑜伽服店发展为全球运动服饰巨头。

Lululemon was founded in Vancouver's Kitsilano neighborhood in 1998, growing from a small yoga shop into a global athletic wear giant.

048

温哥华的冰球是城市最大的体育热情——Canucks（加人队）的比赛日整个城市都沸腾，2011年总决赛失利后甚至引发了骚乱。

Vancouver's passion for ice hockey is the city's biggest sports obsession — on Canucks game days, the entire city buzzes. After the 2011 Stanley Cup Finals loss, the city even erupted in riots.

049

温哥华是全球最宜居城市排行榜的常客，连续多年被《经济学人》评为前五名。

Vancouver is a perennial fixture on "most livable city" rankings, named top five by The Economist for multiple consecutive years.

050

温哥华人有一句自嘲名言："No fun city"——抱怨夜生活不够丰富，酒吧关门太早，娱乐场所太少。

Vancouverites have a self-deprecating nickname for their city: "No Fun City" — complaining about lackluster nightlife, early-closing bars, and too few entertainment venues.

051

温哥华的Wreck Beach是加拿大最著名的天体海滩，位于UBC校园附近，需要走数百级台阶才能到达。

Vancouver's Wreck Beach is Canada's most famous clothing-optional beach, located near UBC campus. Getting there requires descending several hundred steps.

052

温哥华4/20大麻日每年4月20日在Sunset Beach举办大型集会，合法化前就已是北美最大的大麻庆祝活动之一。

Vancouver's 4/20 cannabis rally is held every April 20 at Sunset Beach. Even before legalization, it was one of the largest cannabis celebrations in North America.

经济 & 产业Economy & Industry
053

温哥华港是加拿大最大的港口，也是北美第三大港口，每年处理超过1.4亿吨货物。

Vancouver's port is Canada's largest and North America's third-largest port, handling over 140 million tonnes of cargo annually.

054

温哥华是加拿大影视制作产业的中心，每年影视产业为BC省贡献超过30亿加元的收入。

Vancouver is the center of Canada's film and TV production industry, contributing over $3 billion CAD annually to BC's economy.

055

温哥华的科技产业快速发展，Amazon、Microsoft和Apple在此设立了大型办公室，本地初创公司也层出不穷。

Vancouver's tech industry is growing rapidly. Amazon, Microsoft, and Apple have established major offices here, alongside a thriving local startup scene.

056

温哥华的房价是加拿大最高的，也是全球最难负担的城市之一——普通独立屋均价超过200万加元，公寓均价也超过80万加元。大量外国资本（尤其是来自中国的投资）涌入房地产市场，推高了房价，导致本地年轻人几乎无力购房。温哥华成了"百万富翁住不起的城市"。

Vancouver's housing prices are Canada's highest and among the world's most unaffordable — average detached homes exceed $2 million CAD, and average condos top $800,000 CAD. Massive foreign capital inflows (especially from China) have driven up real estate, making it nearly impossible for young locals to buy homes. Vancouver has become "the city where millionaires can't afford to live."

057

温哥华的矿业虽然不在城市范围内，但大量矿业公司总部设在温哥华，使其成为全球矿业资本的重要集散地。

Vancouver's mining industry isn't within city limits, but numerous mining companies are headquartered here, making it a major global hub for mining capital.

058

温哥华的旅游业每年吸引超过1000万游客，邮轮产业也极为发达，是阿拉斯加邮轮航线的主要出发港。

Vancouver's tourism draws over 10 million visitors annually. The cruise industry is also booming — Vancouver is a major departure port for Alaska cruise routes.

059

温哥华是全球天然气和清洁能源产业的重要融资中心，BC省丰富的水电资源是重要优势。

Vancouver is an important financing center for global natural gas and clean energy industries, with BC's abundant hydroelectric resources as a key advantage.

060

Electronic Arts（EA）在温哥华设有大型工作室，《FIFA》《NHL》等知名游戏在此开发。

Electronic Arts (EA) has a major studio in Vancouver where hit titles like "FIFA" and "NHL" are developed.

061

温哥华的林业是BC省传统支柱产业，但随着环保意识增强和市场变化，产业正在转型。

Vancouver's forestry is a traditional pillar of BC's economy, though the industry is transforming with growing environmental awareness and market shifts.

062

温哥华的三文鱼养殖产业规模庞大，但引发了野生三文鱼保护的激烈争论。

Vancouver's salmon farming industry is massive but has sparked fierce debate about wild salmon conservation.

063

温哥华证券交易所（现并入TMX集团）曾以小型矿业和科技股闻名，也因投机泡沫频发而声名狼藉。

The Vancouver Stock Exchange (now merged into TMX Group) was once known for small-cap mining and tech stocks — and also notorious for frequent speculative bubbles.

064

温哥华的Lululemon、Arc'teryx和Herschel都是本地品牌，从温哥华走向全球，成为城市的商业名片。

Lululemon, Arc'teryx, and Herschel are all Vancouver-born brands that went global, serving as the city's business calling cards.

自然 & 气候Nature & Climate
065

温哥华每年约有166个降雨天，年降水量约1200毫米，雨季从10月持续到次年4月。

Vancouver receives approximately 166 days of rain per year, with about 1,200mm of annual precipitation. The rainy season runs from October through April.

066

温哥华的冬天很少下雪——受太平洋暖流影响，市区冬季平均温度在2-7°C之间，是加拿大最温暖的大城市。

Vancouver's winters rarely see snow — Pacific warm currents keep average winter temperatures between 2-7°C, making it Canada's warmest major city.

067

温哥华周围被温带雨林环绕，Capilano峡谷和Lynn Canyon的古老森林中，有些树木已经生长了800多年。

Vancouver is surrounded by temperate rainforest. In the ancient forests of Capilano Canyon and Lynn Canyon, some trees have been growing for over 800 years.

068

温哥华人对雨有一种独特的文化认同——真正的温哥华人从不打伞，只穿防水外套（最好是Gore-Tex材质）。这不是因为雨不大，而是因为打伞被视为一种"外地人行为"。温哥华的毛毛雨甚至有专门的本地术语——"liquid sunshine"（液体阳光），这种乐观的态度帮助居民度过漫长的雨季。

Vancouverites have a unique cultural relationship with rain — true locals never carry umbrellas, only waterproof jackets (preferably Gore-Tex). This isn't because the rain is light, but because carrying an umbrella is considered "tourist behavior." Vancouver's drizzle even has a local nickname — "liquid sunshine." This optimistic attitude helps residents endure the long rainy season.

069

Capilano吊桥建于1889年，悬挂在70米高的峡谷上方，全长137米，是温哥华最著名的自然景点之一。

The Capilano Suspension Bridge, built in 1889, hangs 70 meters above a canyon and stretches 137 meters — one of Vancouver's most famous natural attractions.

070

每年秋天，温哥华周边河流迎来三文鱼洄游，成千上万的鱼逆流而上产卵，场面壮观。

Every autumn, Vancouver's surrounding rivers welcome the salmon run — thousands of fish swim upstream to spawn, creating a spectacular natural event.

071

温哥华的Grouse Mountain距市中心仅20分钟车程，冬天可滑雪，夏天可徒步，山顶有两只灰熊作为"常驻居民"。

Vancouver's Grouse Mountain is just 20 minutes from downtown. It offers skiing in winter and hiking in summer, with two grizzly bears as "permanent residents" at the summit.

072

温哥华偶尔能看到黑熊和郊狼在城市边缘出没，北温哥华的居民与野生动物共存是日常。

Vancouver occasionally sees black bears and coyotes wandering the city's edges. North Vancouver residents coexist with wildlife as part of daily life.

隐藏细节 & 冷知识Hidden Details & Trivia
073

温哥华的城市名字来自英国探险家George Vancouver船长，他于1792年考察了这片海岸。

Vancouver's city name comes from British explorer George Vancouver, who surveyed these coastlines in 1792.

074

温哥华有一个"Vancouver Special"建筑风格——1960-80年代大量建造的两层平顶住宅，曾被嘲笑为丑陋，如今成了被保护的城市遗产。

Vancouver has a distinctive housing style called the "Vancouver Special" — two-story, flat-roofed homes mass-built from the 1960s to 1980s. Once mocked as ugly, they're now protected as urban heritage.

075

Gastown的蒸汽钟其实主要靠电力驱动，蒸汽只负责产生声音效果和白雾——这个"秘密"让很多游客感到失望。

Gastown's Steam Clock is actually mostly electrically powered. The steam only creates the sound effects and white puffs — a "secret" that disappoints many tourists.

076

温哥华曾在影视作品中扮演过几乎所有城市——因为税收优惠和多样化的地形，温哥华在银幕上曾经是纽约、芝加哥、西雅图、东京甚至外星球。本地人开玩笑说温哥华是"世界上演技最好的城市"——它可以是任何地方，除了它自己。

Vancouver has played almost every city in film and TV — thanks to tax incentives and diverse terrain, it has stood in for New York, Chicago, Seattle, Tokyo, and even alien planets on screen. Locals joke that Vancouver is "the city with the best acting skills in the world" — it can be anywhere except itself.

077

温哥华的English Bay每年新年举办"北极熊冬泳"活动，数千人跳入冰冷的太平洋，传统始于1920年。

Vancouver's English Bay hosts an annual New Year's Day "Polar Bear Swim," where thousands plunge into the freezing Pacific — a tradition dating back to 1920.

078

温哥华不属于温哥华岛——这是很多人常犯的地理错误，温哥华位于大陆，温哥华岛在海峡对面，省会维多利亚在岛上。

Vancouver is not on Vancouver Island — a common geographic mistake. Vancouver is on the mainland; Vancouver Island is across the strait, and the provincial capital Victoria is on the island.

079

温哥华的Stanley Park海堤（Seawall）全长约10公里，是世界上最长的不间断滨水步道之一。

Vancouver's Stanley Park Seawall runs approximately 10 km, making it one of the longest uninterrupted waterfront paths in the world.

080

温哥华的SkyTrain曾经是一个"世博遗产"，最初很多人认为它会失败，如今已成为城市不可或缺的交通大动脉。

Vancouver's SkyTrain was initially an "Expo legacy" that many predicted would fail. Today it's become an indispensable transit backbone for the city.

081

温哥华的Davie Street是LGBTQ+社区的中心，彩虹人行横道是永久性的城市设施。

Vancouver's Davie Street is the center of the LGBTQ+ community. Rainbow crosswalks are a permanent feature of the cityscape.

082

温哥华曾出台"空屋税"（Empty Homes Tax）来打击投机性购房——如果房屋空置超过6个月，需缴纳房价3%的税款。

Vancouver introduced an "Empty Homes Tax" to combat speculative purchases — if a home sits vacant for over 6 months, the owner must pay a tax of 3% of the property value.

083

温哥华的Richmond夜市是北美最大的亚洲夜市之一，夏季开放，摊位上卖的小吃与台湾夜市几乎无异。

Vancouver's Richmond Night Market is one of the largest Asian night markets in North America, open in summer, with food stalls virtually indistinguishable from those at Taiwanese night markets.

084

温哥华的房屋编号系统以Ontario Street为东西分界线，偶数在北侧、奇数在南侧，街道命名从东到西按字母排列。

Vancouver's house numbering system uses Ontario Street as the east-west dividing line. Even numbers are on the north side, odd on the south, and streets are named alphabetically from east to west.

未来 & 争议Future & Controversies
085

温哥华的住房可负担性是全市最大的政治议题——一套普通公寓需要不吃不喝攒30年以上的工资。

Vancouver's housing affordability is the city's biggest political issue — a typical apartment requires over 30 years of salary saved without spending a penny.

086

温哥华Downtown Eastside是加拿大最贫困的社区之一，药物成瘾和无家可归问题极其严重。

Vancouver's Downtown Eastside is one of Canada's poorest neighborhoods, with severe drug addiction and homelessness problems.

087

温哥华是全球第一个设立合法安全注射站（Insite）的城市，试图用减害策略应对阿片类药物危机。

Vancouver was the first city in the world to establish a legal safe injection site (Insite), using harm reduction strategies to address the opioid crisis.

088

温哥华的"外国买家禁令"和各种房产税政策引发了激烈争论——支持者认为这是保护本地居民住房权的必要措施，反对者则认为这带有排外色彩且效果有限。温哥华房地产已经不仅是经济问题，更是文化身份和移民政策的交汇点，每次选举都成为核心议题。

Vancouver's "foreign buyer ban" and various property taxes have sparked fierce debate — supporters see them as necessary to protect local housing rights, while opponents call them xenophobic and ineffective. Vancouver real estate has become more than an economic issue — it's a flashpoint for cultural identity and immigration policy, dominating every election cycle.

089

温哥华计划到2050年成为全球最绿色的城市，包括实现零碳排放、100%使用可再生能源。

Vancouver aims to become the world's greenest city by 2050, including achieving zero carbon emissions and 100% renewable energy.

090

温哥华面临大地震的威胁——Cascadia俯冲带每隔约500年发生一次9级大地震，上一次是1700年，下一次随时可能到来。

Vancouver faces a major earthquake threat — the Cascadia Subduction Zone produces a magnitude 9 quake roughly every 500 years. The last was in 1700; the next could come at any time.

091

温哥华的Broadway地铁延长线正在建设中，将大幅改善UBC和市中心之间的交通，预计2026年通车。

Vancouver's Broadway Subway extension is under construction, set to vastly improve transit between UBC and downtown, with completion expected in 2026.

092

温哥华的天然气管道扩建项目（Trans Mountain）引发了激烈的环保争议和原住民抗议。

Vancouver's Trans Mountain pipeline expansion project has sparked intense environmental protests and Indigenous opposition.

093

温哥华的唐人街正在经历快速的士绅化，老建筑被拆除建高楼，传统社区文化面临消失的危险。

Vancouver's Chinatown is experiencing rapid gentrification. Old buildings are being demolished for towers, and traditional community culture faces the threat of extinction.

094

温哥华大量住宅面积越来越小——为了应对高房价，开发商推出了大量300-400平方英尺的微型公寓。

Vancouver's apartment sizes keep shrinking — to cope with high prices, developers offer micro-units of 300-400 square feet.

095

温哥华的气候变化影响日益明显，2021年的"热穹顶"事件使温度飙升至49.6°C，打破了加拿大历史纪录。

Vancouver's climate change impacts are increasingly evident. The 2021 "heat dome" event sent temperatures soaring to 49.6°C, shattering Canada's all-time record.

096

尽管房价高企、雨天漫长，温哥华人依然热爱这座城市——山、海、雨林和多元文化是他们选择留下的理由。

Despite sky-high housing prices and endless rain, Vancouverites love their city — mountains, sea, rainforest, and multiculturalism are why they choose to stay.

本地人才知道的事Only Locals Know
097

温哥华本地人从不打伞——只穿防水外套。如果你在街上看到有人撑伞，那几乎可以确定是游客。

True Vancouverites never carry umbrellas — just a waterproof jacket. If you see someone with an umbrella on the street, they're almost certainly a tourist.

098

真正的温哥华人知道最好的寿司不在市中心，而是在Kingsway大道沿线那些不起眼的小店里。

Real Vancouverites know the best sushi isn't downtown — it's at unassuming little spots along Kingsway.

099

温哥华人衡量距离的方式不是公里而是时间——"那个地方大概20分钟"，因为在这座城市里，实际距离和通勤时间完全是两回事。

Vancouverites measure distance not in kilometers but in time — "That place is about 20 minutes away," because in this city, actual distance and commute time are completely different things.

100

温哥华人最常抱怨的三件事：房价、雨天和交通——但如果有人建议他们搬去别的城市，他们会立刻反驳："你疯了吗？这里可是温哥华。"

The three things Vancouverites complain about most: housing prices, rain, and traffic — but if anyone suggests they move to another city, they'll instantly fire back: "Are you crazy? This is Vancouver."
