地理 & 城市结构Geography & Urban Structure
001

新奥尔良位于海平面以下，城市大部分区域比密西西比河水位还低，完全依赖堤坝和水泵系统维持干燥。

New Orleans sits largely below sea level — the city is essentially a bowl surrounded by the Mississippi River, Lake Pontchartrain, and swamps. Some neighborhoods sit up to 3 meters below sea level.

002

新奥尔良的形状像一个弯月，因此被称为"Crescent City"（新月之城），密西西比河在此拐弯入海。

The city is built on the Mississippi River delta, on land composed of river sediment that continues to sink. This subsidence, combined with rising sea levels, makes New Orleans one of the most vulnerable major cities in the US.

003

法语区（French Quarter）是城市最古老的街区，但现存建筑大多是西班牙殖民时期的风格，而非法式。

The French Quarter (Vieux Carré) is the oldest neighborhood in New Orleans, though most of its buildings are actually Spanish colonial in style — two devastating fires during Spain's rule led to a complete rebuilding.

004

新奥尔良的花园区（Garden District）以19世纪美国富商的豪宅著称，橡树林荫道和铁艺围栏是标志景观。

New Orleans is shaped like a crescent hugging a bend in the Mississippi River, which earned it the nickname "Crescent City." The river's curve also means that "north" and "south" directions are virtually meaningless here — locals use "lakeside" and "riverside" instead.

005

新奥尔良正在缓慢下沉——由于地下水抽取、沼泽地基压缩等原因，城市每年下沉约2-5厘米。加上海平面上升和飓风威胁，这座城市的长期存续面临根本性挑战。很多科学家预测，如果不采取激进措施，新奥尔良可能在本世纪末部分被淹没。

New Orleans' geography is fundamentally defined by water management — the city relies on an elaborate system of levees, pumps, and canals to keep the Mississippi and Lake Pontchartrain from reclaiming the land. The pumping system can move over 30 billion gallons of water per day. Without constant human intervention, New Orleans would literally disappear underwater within weeks.

006

新奥尔良被庞恰特雷恩湖（Lake Pontchartrain）环抱，这实际上是一个潟湖，与墨西哥湾相连。

The Garden District features some of America's finest antebellum mansions, with live oaks draped in Spanish moss lining the streets. The neighborhood's name comes from the elaborate gardens that wealthy Americans cultivated to distinguish themselves from the Creole French Quarter residents.

007

城市的街道名保留了大量法语和西班牙语遗迹：Bourbon（波旁）、Royal（皇家）、Chartres（沙特尔）等。

Lake Pontchartrain is not actually a lake but a tidal lagoon connected to the Gulf of Mexico. Its causeway, at 38.6 km, is one of the longest bridges over water in the world.

008

新奥尔良的墓地被称为"死亡之城"，由于地下水位高，棺材会浮起，所以全部采用地上墓葬。

The city's above-ground cemeteries earned New Orleans the nickname "City of the Dead." Because the high water table prevents traditional burial, the dead are interred in elaborate tombs that look like miniature houses.

「在新奥尔良，葬礼是一场游行，
派对是一种信仰，
悲伤和欢乐从来不分家。」

交通 & 出行
009

新奥尔良的有轨电车（Streetcar）是全美最古老的公共交通系统之一，圣查尔斯线自1835年运营至今。

New Orleans' streetcar system is one of the oldest continuously operating in the world. The St. Charles line has been running since 1835 — its olive-green cars are the city's most beloved form of transit.

010

电车是田纳西·威廉斯名剧《欲望号街车》的灵感来源，"Desire"实际上是一条已停运的电车线路名。

The Pontchartrain Causeway stretches 38.6 km across Lake Pontchartrain. For much of the drive, no land is visible in any direction — a disorienting experience for first-time crossers.

011

新奥尔良的公共交通整体落后，很多人依赖私家车，这也是卡特里娜飓风时很多穷人无法撤离的原因之一。

New Orleans' streets are notoriously potholed. The combination of sinking soil, aging infrastructure, and frequent flooding means roads are in constant disrepair. Locals joke that driving in New Orleans is like an obstacle course.

012

新奥尔良的道路状况极差，坑洼遍地，当地人戏称开车像在月球表面行驶。

New Orleans' unique relationship with water extends to its drainage system — the city depends on massive pumping stations to remove rainwater, since gravity alone can't drain water from a bowl-shaped city. When pumps fail during heavy rain, flooding is immediate and severe. This fragile infrastructure was catastrophically exposed during Hurricane Katrina.

013

法语区是步行者天堂，大部分景点、酒吧、餐厅都在步行范围内，不需要开车。

The Mississippi River flows right through the city but sits higher than the surrounding neighborhoods. The levees that contain it are literally above the rooftops of nearby houses — an unnerving reality for residents.

014

密西西比河上的渡轮免费运营，连接新奥尔良与西岸的阿尔及尔角，是欣赏城市天际线的好方式。

Louis Armstrong International Airport was renamed in 2001 to honor New Orleans' most famous son. The new terminal, opened in 2019, is a dramatic improvement over the infamously decrepit old one.

015

新奥尔良机场名为路易斯·阿姆斯特朗国际机场，以爵士乐传奇命名，是全美唯一以音乐家命名的主要机场。

New Orleans is one of the few US cities where walking and biking are genuinely viable transportation — the compact French Quarter and surrounding neighborhoods are easily navigable on foot.

016

新奥尔良是美国少数几个允许在公共场所饮酒的城市之一，走在街上手拿鸡尾酒完全合法。

New Orleans has the most festivals per capita of any American city — over 130 annually, including Mardi Gras, Jazz Fest, Essence Festival, and dozens of neighborhood celebrations.

食物 & 饮食文化Food & Dining Culture
017

新奥尔良菜融合了法国、西班牙、非洲、加勒比多元影响，形成独特的克里奥尔（Creole）和卡津（Cajun）风味。

Gumbo is New Orleans' signature dish — a thick stew with African, French, and Native American influences. Every family has their own recipe, and the debate between okra-based and filé-based gumbo is one of the city's great culinary arguments.

018

Gumbo是新奥尔良的招牌菜，一种浓稠的炖汤，用秋葵、香肠、海鲜或鸡肉熬制，配米饭食用。

Beignets from Café du Monde are perhaps New Orleans' most famous food item — square French-style doughnuts buried under a mountain of powdered sugar, served 24 hours a day alongside café au lait.

019

Jambalaya是另一道经典料理，类似西班牙海鲜饭，混合了香肠、虾、蔬菜和米饭。

The po'boy sandwich was invented during a 1929 streetcar workers' strike. The owners of Martin Brothers' restaurant served free sandwiches to the "poor boys" on strike. The overstuffed sandwich — typically filled with fried shrimp, oysters, or roast beef — became New Orleans' iconic casual meal.

020

新奥尔良的Po'boy三明治是城市灵魂美食——法式面包夹炸虾、炸牡蛎或其他海鲜，起源于1929年大罢工期间，当时有餐厅免费给罢工工人提供食物，称他们为"poor boys"，后简化为Po'boy。

New Orleans' food culture is deeply rooted in Creole and Cajun traditions — Creole cooking is the city-based, refined style influenced by French, Spanish, African, and Caribbean cuisines, while Cajun cooking comes from the rural French-speaking Acadians of the bayou. Many dishes commonly attributed to one tradition actually draw from both. Jambalaya, crawfish étouffée, red beans and rice, and muffuletta sandwiches are all unique to this region and cannot be authentically replicated anywhere else.

021

Beignet是新奥尔良的法式甜甜圈，撒上厚厚的糖粉，Café du Monde的beignet是游客必打卡项目。

Crawfish boil season (late winter through early summer) is a citywide celebration. Families and friends gather outdoors to boil pounds of crawfish with potatoes, corn, and heavy Cajun seasoning — it's as much social ritual as it is a meal.

022

新奥尔良是小龙虾之都，每年消费的小龙虾数以百万磅计，Crawfish Boil（煮小龙虾派对）是当地社交活动。

New Orleans' cocktail culture is legendary — the city claims to have invented the cocktail itself. The Sazerac (rye, absinthe, bitters) is the official cocktail of New Orleans, and the Hurricane (rum, passion fruit) is the tourist favorite.

023

新奥尔良的鸡尾酒文化举世闻名，Sazerac被认为是美国第一款鸡尾酒，Hurricane和Hand Grenade是当地特色。

Red beans and rice is the traditional Monday dish — housewives would put beans on to simmer while doing laundry on Mondays (historically the busiest housework day). Louis Armstrong used to sign his letters "Red beans and ricely yours."

024

新奥尔良每周五必吃海鲜，这是天主教斋戒传统的遗留，很多餐厅周五有特别的海鲜套餐。

New Orleans' pralines (pecan candies) are a Creole creation adapted from the French original, substituting locally abundant pecans for almonds. They're sold at candy shops throughout the French Quarter.

历史 & 政治History & Politics
025

新奥尔良由法国人建于1718年，以法国摄政王奥尔良公爵命名，后被转让给西班牙，再卖给美国。

Jean Lafitte, the legendary pirate and privateer, operated out of the New Orleans bayous in the early 1800s. He helped Andrew Jackson defend the city during the Battle of New Orleans in 1815 and received a presidential pardon for his services.

026

1803年的路易斯安那购地案是美国历史上最划算的交易——美国以1500万美元从拿破仑手中买下整个路易斯安那领地。

The Louisiana Purchase of 1803 — in which the US bought New Orleans and 828,000 square miles of territory from Napoleon for $15 million — is considered the greatest real estate deal in history, effectively doubling the size of the United States.

027

新奥尔良曾是美国最大的奴隶市场，成千上万的非洲奴隶在这里被买卖，这段历史深刻塑造了城市的文化和人口构成。

New Orleans was the largest slave market in the antebellum South. The city's wealth was built on the labor of enslaved people, and this history profoundly shapes the city's culture, demographics, and ongoing racial dynamics to this day.

028

新奥尔良有独特的"自由有色人"（Free People of Color）群体，他们在奴隶制时代就是自由身，形成了独特的克里奥尔文化。

The Battle of New Orleans (January 1815) was the final major battle of the War of 1812 — and was actually fought two weeks after the peace treaty had been signed, because news traveled slowly. Andrew Jackson's decisive victory made him a national hero and eventually president.

029

1815年的新奥尔良战役是英美战争的最后一战，安德鲁·杰克逊在此击败英军，成为战争英雄，后当选总统。

New Orleans' Creole culture is unique in America — a complex blend of French, Spanish, African, Caribbean, and Catholic traditions that produced a distinct language (Louisiana Creole), cuisine, music, and social structure found nowhere else in the world.

030

新奥尔良长期是腐败之城，政治机器、黑帮、贿赂是城市政治的常态，"路易斯安那政治"成为美国政治腐败的代名词。

The Storyville red-light district, legal from 1897 to 1917, was the birthplace of jazz. Musicians like Jelly Roll Morton and King Oliver played in the district's clubs and brothels, developing the improvisational style that would become America's greatest musical contribution to the world.

031

2005年的卡特里娜飓风是新奥尔良历史上最惨痛的灾难——堤坝决口，80%的城市被淹，1800多人死亡，数十万人流离失所。联邦政府的救援失败暴露了美国体制的深层问题。至今，很多居民仍未返回，城市人口比飓风前减少了约20%。

Mardi Gras has been celebrated in New Orleans since 1699. The two-week festival culminates on Fat Tuesday with elaborate parades, masked balls, and the famous throwing of beads, doubloons, and cups from floats.

032

新奥尔良黑人人口占总人口约60%，是美国黑人文化最浓郁的城市之一，但种族隔离和不平等问题至今存在。

The 2005 Hurricane Katrina and the subsequent levee failures killed over 1,800 people and displaced hundreds of thousands. It was the deadliest and costliest natural disaster in modern US history, exposing catastrophic failures in engineering, emergency response, and racial equity.

建筑 & 空间Architecture & Space
033

法语区的铁艺阳台是城市标志性景观，实际上是西班牙殖民时期引入的，法国人原本不用铁艺装饰。

Jackson Square in the French Quarter is named after Andrew Jackson and features the iconic St. Louis Cathedral — the oldest continuously active cathedral in the United States, consecrated in 1794.

034

新奥尔良的房屋有独特的"Shotgun House"风格——狭长的单层木屋，据说从前门开枪可以直接穿过后门。

St. Louis Cemetery No. 1 is the city's oldest existing cemetery (1789) and home to the alleged tomb of voodoo queen Marie Laveau. Due to vandalism, it's now accessible only by guided tour.

035

圣路易大教堂是美国最古老的天主教堂之一，建于1718年，是法语区杰克逊广场的地标。

The Superdome (now Caesars Superdome) is one of the largest domed structures in the world. During Katrina, it served as a shelter of last resort for over 20,000 people in horrific conditions — images that shocked the world.

036

花园区的橡树林荫道（Oak Alley）是南方种植园建筑的经典代表，28棵300年橡树形成壮观的隧道。

New Orleans' shotgun houses are the city's most distinctive residential architecture — long, narrow houses with rooms arranged one behind another, without hallways. The name supposedly comes from the idea that you could fire a shotgun through the front door and the bullet would exit the back without hitting a wall.

037

新奥尔良的超级穹顶体育馆在卡特里娜飓风期间成为避难所，但条件恶劣，数万人在此度过噩梦般的数天。

The French Market in the French Quarter has operated continuously since 1791, making it the oldest public market in the United States. It now features a flea market, food stalls, and artisan vendors.

038

保存大厅（Preservation Hall）是新奥尔良最著名的爵士乐酒吧，自1961年营业至今，没有空调、没有酒水，只有纯粹的音乐。

Magazine Street stretches 10 km through some of New Orleans' most charming neighborhoods, lined with antique shops, galleries, boutiques, and restaurants. It's the city's best shopping corridor.

039

新奥尔良的拉斐特公墓是全美最上镜的墓地之一，出现在无数电影和电视剧中，是哥特式浪漫的象征。

The Preservation Hall is a cramped, no-frills music venue in the French Quarter dedicated exclusively to traditional New Orleans jazz. There are no drinks, no food, no air conditioning — just music so pure it brings audiences to tears nightly.

040

卡特里娜后的重建是一个复杂的故事——一些街区得到修复甚至绅士化，房价飙升，原住民被迫迁出；另一些街区至今仍是废墟，杂草丛生。这种不均衡的复苏反映了美国社会的深层不平等。

New Orleans' wrought-iron balconies in the French Quarter are actually Spanish, not French — they were built after the fires of 1788 and 1794 destroyed the original French colonial buildings. The elaborate ironwork has become the city's most photographed architectural feature.

文化 & 社会Culture & Society
041

新奥尔良是爵士乐的诞生地，19世纪末20世纪初，非洲节奏、欧洲和声和蓝调在这里融合，创造了全新的音乐形式。

Jazz was born in New Orleans in the early 1900s, emerging from the intersection of African rhythms, European harmonies, and Caribbean musical traditions. Louis Armstrong, born in a New Orleans slum in 1901, became the genre's greatest ambassador.

042

路易斯·阿姆斯特朗是新奥尔良最伟大的音乐家，他从贫民区走出，成为全球爵士乐的象征。

Mardi Gras Indians are African American neighborhood groups that create spectacular hand-sewn beaded suits (costing thousands of dollars and taking a full year to make) and parade on Mardi Gras and St. Joseph's Day. This tradition blends African, Caribbean, and Native American cultural elements.

043

狂欢节（Mardi Gras）是新奥尔良最盛大的年度庆典，每年吸引数百万游客，持续数周的游行、派对和狂欢。

New Orleans' second-line parades are spontaneous street celebrations where a brass band leads and the "second line" of dancers and revelers follows. They happen nearly every weekend and are the city's most authentic cultural expression.

044

狂欢节的彩珠（beads）是标志性元素，游行队伍向人群抛撒彩珠，接到越多越幸运。

New Orleans is the birthplace of brass band culture — the Rebirth Brass Band, Hot 8 Brass Band, and Preservation Hall Jazz Band keep the tradition alive. The sound of a brass band echoing through the streets is the sonic signature of the city.

045

新奥尔良的葬礼游行（Second Line）是独特的文化传统——送葬队伍伴随铜管乐队前行，悲伤的哀乐逐渐转为欢快的爵士乐。

Voodoo (more accurately, Vodou) is a genuine religious practice in New Orleans, blending West African spiritual traditions with Catholicism. Marie Laveau, the 19th-century "Voodoo Queen," remains the city's most legendary figure. The New Orleans Historic Voodoo Museum offers insight into a tradition that is far more complex and spiritual than Hollywood portrayals suggest.

046

新奥尔良有浓厚的巫毒教（Voodoo）传统，融合了非洲宗教与天主教元素，玛丽·拉沃是最著名的巫毒女王。

New Orleans' literary tradition is extraordinary — Tennessee Williams wrote "A Streetcar Named Desire" here, William Faulkner lived in the French Quarter, and Anne Rice set her vampire novels in the city's Gothic landscape.

047

新奥尔良人以"laissez les bons temps rouler"（让美好时光滚动起来）为座右铭，享乐主义是城市的核心价值。

New Orleans' LGBTQ+ community is one of the most vibrant in the South. The annual Southern Decadence festival draws over 200,000 participants and is the largest LGBTQ+ event in the region.

048

新奥尔良是美国LGBTQ文化最开放的南方城市，每年的Southern Decadence是全美最大的同志庆典之一。这种开放态度与天主教传统、南方保守主义并存，形成了新奥尔良独特的"矛盾共存"文化。

Bourbon Street is simultaneously New Orleans' most famous and most despised street — tourists love its neon-lit bars and open-container policy, while locals avoid it entirely, viewing it as a debased caricature of the city's real culture.

049

新奥尔良的社交俱乐部（Social Aid and Pleasure Clubs）是非裔社区的重要组织，负责葬礼、游行和社区互助。

New Orleans celebrates death differently — jazz funerals feature a brass band playing somber hymns on the way to the cemetery and upbeat, dancing music on the way back. The tradition transforms grief into celebration, reflecting the city's deeply ingrained philosophy that life should be celebrated at every turn.

050

新奥尔良人对大学橄榄球的热情不亚于任何南方城市，LSU（路易斯安那州立大学）的比赛日全城沸腾。

Lundi Gras (the Monday before Mardi Gras) features the arrival of Rex, the King of Carnival, by riverboat — a tradition that dates back to 1874 and marks the beginning of the final 36 hours of Carnival.

「新奥尔良是美国最不像美国的城市，
这里有自己的时区，
自己的规则，自己的灵魂。」

自然 & 动物
051

新奥尔良周边是密西西比河三角洲湿地，这片沼泽地是美国最重要的生态系统之一，但正在以惊人速度消失。

The New Orleans Saints football team and the Superdome are central to the city's identity. The Saints' Super Bowl victory in 2010 — coming just years after Katrina — was seen as a moment of spiritual redemption for the entire city.

052

路易斯安那州的鳄鱼数量超过200万只，在新奥尔良郊外的沼泽地很容易看到野生鳄鱼。

Bourbon Street's name has nothing to do with bourbon whiskey — it's named after the French royal House of Bourbon. However, the street has fully embraced the whiskey association for marketing purposes.

053

新奥尔良的飓风季从6月持续到11月，每年都有多次热带风暴威胁，居民已经习惯了"撤离或硬扛"的生活。

The tourism industry is New Orleans' largest employer, generating over $10 billion annually. The city hosts approximately 18 million visitors per year, with Mardi Gras alone bringing in over 1.4 million.

054

新奥尔良的湿度极高，夏季闷热难耐，体感温度常常超过40°C，空调是生存必需品。

New Orleans' port is the sixth-largest in the US by tonnage. Its strategic position at the mouth of the Mississippi makes it a crucial gateway for agricultural exports from the entire Midwest.

055

新奥尔良的蚊子堪称恐怖，尤其在雨后，户外活动需要大量驱蚊剂保护。

The oil and gas industry has historically been a major employer, with many energy companies headquartered in New Orleans. The 2010 Deepwater Horizon disaster, just offshore, devastated the local fishing and tourism industries.

056

路易斯安那的棕色鹈鹕是州鸟，也是NBA新奥尔良鹈鹕队的命名来源。

New Orleans' film industry has earned Louisiana the nickname "Hollywood South." Tax incentives have attracted major productions, and the city's atmospheric architecture provides ready-made sets for period dramas and thrillers.

经济 & 财富
057

新奥尔良港是美国最繁忙的港口之一，处理大量石油、谷物和集装箱货物。

New Orleans' economy is heavily dependent on tourism and hospitality, making it extremely vulnerable to disruptions. The COVID-19 pandemic devastated the city's economy — Bourbon Street stood empty, hotels closed, and tens of thousands of service workers lost their jobs overnight.

058

旅游业是新奥尔良经济支柱，每年吸引超过1800万游客，贡献数十亿美元收入。

New Orleans' creative economy — music, art, food, and cultural tourism — generates billions but often fails to adequately compensate the Black artists and culture bearers who create and sustain these traditions. The tension between cultural celebration and economic exploitation is a defining issue.

059

新奥尔良的石油和天然气产业曾是经济命脉，但随着能源转型，这一产业正在萎缩。

New Orleans has one of the highest poverty rates among major US cities — approximately 25% of residents live below the poverty line, with rates even higher in predominantly Black neighborhoods.

060

新奥尔良的贫困率在美国大城市中名列前茅，约25%的居民生活在贫困线以下。卡特里娜后的重建主要惠及了游客区和富人区，而贫困社区的改善有限。这座派对之城的背后是严峻的社会不平等。

The cost of living in New Orleans is lower than most major US cities, making it attractive to artists and young professionals. However, post-Katrina gentrification has rapidly driven up rents in historically affordable neighborhoods.

061

新奥尔良的餐饮业是重要雇主，从高档餐厅到街边小摊，数万人靠食物谋生。

New Orleans' climate is subtropical — summers are brutally hot and humid (regularly exceeding 35°C with 90%+ humidity), while winters are mild with occasional cool snaps. The heat from June to September is genuinely oppressive.

062

新奥尔良的房价在卡特里娜后经历了剧烈波动，如今热门街区的价格已超过飓风前，但很多区域仍是空地。

Hurricane season runs from June 1 to November 30, and New Orleans lives in annual dread. Besides Katrina (2005), Hurricane Ida (2021) caused severe damage, and the city faces increasingly frequent and intense storms due to climate change.

日常生活 & 城市习惯
063

新奥尔良人以"慢"著称，一切都不着急，约会迟到30分钟被称为"新奥尔良时间"。

New Orleans receives about 1,600mm of rainfall annually — one of the wettest major cities in the US. Afternoon thunderstorms in summer are almost daily events, dropping torrential rain that overwhelms the drainage system within minutes.

064

新奥尔良人的招呼语是"Where y'at?"，意思是"你好/怎么样？"，这也是当地人被称为"Yat"的原因。

New Orleans is sinking at an alarming rate — some areas are subsiding by up to 50mm per year. Combined with rising sea levels, this means the city's long-term survival is genuinely in question. Engineers and planners are racing to develop solutions, but the fundamental challenge of maintaining a city below sea level in an era of climate change may ultimately prove insurmountable.

065

新奥尔良人爱喝酒，白天喝酒完全正常，很多人从brunch就开始，持续到深夜。

The bayous and swamps surrounding New Orleans are home to alligators, nutria, and incredible bird diversity. Swamp tours departing from the city's outskirts are among the most popular non-urban attractions.

066

新奥尔良的酒吧永不关门——城市没有强制酒吧打烊时间，很多酒吧24小时营业。

New Orleans' live oaks are natural monuments — some are over 500 years old, with sprawling canopies draped in Spanish moss. City Park's Singing Oak has wind chimes hung in its branches, creating natural music.

067

新奥尔良人极其友善，陌生人之间打招呼、聊天是常态，与美国北方的冷漠形成鲜明对比。

Audubon Park and Audubon Zoo, named after naturalist John James Audubon who lived in New Orleans, occupy 140 hectares along the Mississippi. The zoo specializes in Louisiana wildlife, including its famous white alligators.

068

新奥尔良的周日通常从brunch开始，配上血腥玛丽或含羞草鸡尾酒，然后是爵士乐和闲逛。

The Mississippi River at New Orleans is approximately 60 meters deep — deep enough for ocean-going container ships. Watching massive cargo vessels pass just meters from the French Quarter levee is a surreal New Orleans experience.

069

新奥尔良人对"圣徒队"（NFL橄榄球队）的热爱近乎宗教——2009年圣徒队首次赢得超级碗时，整座城市陷入狂欢，那一刻被视为卡特里娜后城市复兴的象征。比赛日全城放假，教堂提前结束礼拜，一切为橄榄球让路。

New Orleans is one of the most haunted cities in America — ghost tours are a major tourist industry, trading on stories of voodoo, yellow fever epidemics, and centuries of dramatic deaths in a city that has always had an intimate relationship with mortality.

070

新奥尔良人有独特的"lagniappe"传统，意思是"多给一点"——商家常常会给顾客额外的小礼物或折扣。

The Krewe of Rex, founded in 1872, established many Mardi Gras traditions including the official colors (purple for justice, gold for power, green for faith) and the custom of throwing trinkets from floats.

奇闻 & 冷知识
071

新奥尔良的波旁街（Bourbon Street）每天凌晨都会被水枪冲洗，洗掉前一夜的酒精和呕吐物。

The famous "Go cups" are unique to New Orleans — bars will pour your drink into a plastic cup when you leave so you can keep drinking on the street. Open-container laws barely exist here, giving the city a perpetual party atmosphere.

072

狂欢节的国王蛋糕（King Cake）里藏着一个塑料婴儿，找到的人要负责买下一个蛋糕。

New Orleans' music clubs charge little or no cover — Frenchmen Street, not Bourbon Street, is where locals go for live music. On any given night, you can hear jazz, funk, brass band, zydeco, and blues within a three-block stretch.

073

新奥尔良的驱魔仪式至今仍在进行，天主教会每年处理数十起"驱魔"请求。

Frenchmen Street in the Faubourg Marigny is the soul of New Orleans nightlife. While Bourbon Street caters to tourists with karaoke and frozen daiquiris, Frenchmen offers world-class live music in intimate, sweaty clubs where the musicians play for the love of it.

074

新奥尔良的圣约翰前夜（St. John's Eve）是巫毒教的重要节日，信徒在庞恰特雷恩湖边举行仪式。

New Orleans' relationship with race is more complex than any other American city. The unique Creole category — neither simply Black nor White — created a three-tiered racial system that defied the rest of America's binary. Today, the city remains deeply segregated economically while being culturally integrated in ways that other Southern cities are not.

075

新奥尔良的鬼故事极其丰富，几乎每栋老建筑都有自己的闹鬼传说，鬼魂之旅是热门旅游项目。

Post-Katrina gentrification has transformed neighborhoods like Bywater and the Marigny — areas that were working-class and predominantly Black are now home to coffee shops, galleries, and Airbnbs catering to a wealthier, whiter demographic. Long-term residents have been priced out of the neighborhoods their families built.

076

新奥尔良有全美唯一的"吸血鬼酒吧"和"吸血鬼社群"——一群自称吸血鬼的人定期聚会、喝血（自愿捐献）。安妮·赖斯的《夜访吸血鬼》就以新奥尔良为背景，使这座城市成为吸血鬼文化的圣地。

New Orleans' Airbnb explosion has been controversial — entire neighborhoods have been converted from residential to short-term rental properties, reducing housing stock and changing the character of historic communities.

077

新奥尔良的Krewe（狂欢节社团）有严格的等级制度，加入顶级Krewe需要数代人的社会地位积累。

New Orleans' public education system was almost entirely replaced by charter schools after Katrina — the most radical education reform experiment in US history. Results are mixed and heavily debated.

078

新奥尔良没有"last call"——酒吧不会提前喊"最后一杯"，因为根本不需要关门。

New Orleans' infrastructure is in perpetual crisis — water mains break daily, streets crumble, and the drainage system requires constant repair. The city estimates $10+ billion in deferred maintenance.

079

新奥尔良的午夜大弥撒（Midnight Mass）在圣诞节凌晨举行，是天主教传统与狂欢文化的奇妙融合。

New Orleans' resilience is its defining quality. Through slavery, yellow fever epidemics, floods, and hurricanes, the city has refused to die. Every time it's knocked down, it gets back up, turns on the music, and pours another drink.

080

新奥尔良人把"neutral ground"（马路中央的绿化带）当作聚会场所，野餐、喝酒、听音乐都在这里进行。

New Orleans locals never go to Bourbon Street — the real city lives on Frenchmen, Magazine, Oak Street, and in the neighborhood bars where everybody knows your name and the bartender pours heavy.

艺术 & 创作社群
081

新奥尔良是爵士乐、布鲁斯、放克、R&B的发源地或重镇，几乎影响了所有美国流行音乐。

In New Orleans, the proper response to "Where y'at?" (the local greeting) is not a location but a state of being: "Alright!" This phrase is so central to local identity that lifelong residents are called "Yats."

082

Fats Domino、Dr. John、Wynton Marsalis都是新奥尔良音乐传奇，他们的音乐定义了城市的声音。

New Orleanians will passionately debate the best snowball stand (shaved ice, not snow cones — there's a crucial difference). Hansen's Sno-Bliz, open since 1939, is the gold standard, and locals will drive 30 minutes across town for one.

083

新奥尔良的爵士乐葬礼不是表演，而是真正的社区传统，至今仍在非裔社区中延续。

The ultimate New Orleans wisdom: "Laissez les bons temps rouler" (Let the good times roll). It's not just a party slogan — it's a philosophy of life that says joy, music, food, and human connection aren't luxuries to be earned but rights to be exercised daily, especially in the face of adversity.

084

田纳西·威廉斯在新奥尔良完成了《欲望号街车》，剧中的场景就是法语区的街道和公寓。

The New Orleans Jazz & Heritage Festival (Jazz Fest) is one of the world's premier music festivals, drawing over 400,000 attendees over two weekends each spring. It showcases not just jazz but blues, R&B, gospel, Cajun, zydeco, and rock.

085

新奥尔良的Mardi Gras Indians是独特的文化现象——非裔美国人身着华丽的手工羽毛服装，在狂欢节游行。这一传统源于非洲奴隶对土著印第安人庇护的感恩，如今已成为城市最绚丽的文化表达，每套服装需要一整年手工制作。

New Orleans' Mardi Gras krewes are private social organizations that fund and organize the parades. Some, like Rex and Comus, date back to the 1850s. Membership in elite krewes is passed down through families like heirlooms.

086

新奥尔良的街头艺人是城市景观的一部分，法语区的街头到处是铜管乐队、塔罗牌占卜师和人体雕塑。

The French Quarter Festival is considered by locals to be superior to Jazz Fest — it's free, less crowded, and features primarily local musicians. It's the largest free music festival in the South.

教育 & 知识
087

杜兰大学是新奥尔良最著名的私立大学，以医学院和商学院闻名，是"南方常春藤"之一。

New Orleans has the highest per-capita number of musicians of any American city. Yet most working musicians struggle to make a living wage — the average New Orleans musician earns less than $20,000 annually from music.

088

泽维尔大学是美国唯一的黑人天主教大学，培养的非裔医生数量全美第一。

New Orleans' Tremé neighborhood is the oldest African American neighborhood in the United States. It was the birthplace of jazz and remains a vibrant center of Black culture, though gentrification pressures are mounting.

089

卡特里娜后，新奥尔良公立学校系统几乎完全转型为特许学校，成为美国教育改革的实验场，争议至今不断。

The levee system was rebuilt after Katrina at a cost of $14.5 billion — the largest civil works project in US history. Whether it will hold against the next major hurricane remains the city's existential question.

090

新奥尔良的国家二战博物馆被评为全美最佳博物馆之一，是了解二战历史的重要场所。

New Orleans' coastal erosion crisis is dire — Louisiana loses approximately a football field of wetland every 100 minutes. These wetlands serve as natural hurricane buffers; as they disappear, the city becomes more vulnerable.

身为新奥尔良人
091

新奥尔良人有一种独特的韧性——经历了飓风、洪水、疫情，他们总能在废墟上重建派对。

The Sewerage & Water Board of New Orleans is one of the most criticized agencies in the city — aging pumps, turbines from the 1910s, and chronic power failures have made drainage unreliable during the storms that matter most.

092

新奥尔良人对音乐的热爱是骨子里的，几乎每个人都会某种乐器，或者至少能跳几步。

Post-Katrina New Orleans has become a laboratory for urban experimentation — charter schools, innovative housing designs, community land trusts, and cultural preservation programs have all been tested here, with mixed results.

093

新奥尔良人把食物当作一种语言——聊天总是从"你吃了什么？"开始，食谱是家族传承的秘密。

New Orleans' short-term rental boom has hollowed out residential neighborhoods. In some blocks of the Marigny and Bywater, more than half the houses are Airbnbs, turning communities into de facto hotels.

094

新奥尔良人对邻里关系极其重视，大家认识彼此的名字，社区意识比美国大多数城市都强。

The racial wealth gap in New Orleans is among the worst in the country. White households have, on average, 10 times the wealth of Black households — a legacy of slavery, segregation, and systematic disinvestment.

095

新奥尔良人不喜欢连锁店，更愿意支持本地小店，这也是城市保持独特性的原因之一。

New Orleans' murder rate has historically been among the highest in the US, driven by poverty, gun accessibility, and inadequate social services. Yet violent crime is heavily concentrated in specific neighborhoods — the French Quarter and Garden District are among the safest areas in the city.

096

新奥尔良人对卡特里娜有复杂的情感——它是创伤，也是身份的一部分，"你在哪里度过飓风？"是常见的对话开场白。

Despite everything, New Orleans continues to attract newcomers — the city's cultural richness, low cost of living (by big-city standards), and irresistible joie de vivre draw artists, musicians, chefs, and dreamers from around the world.

097

新奥尔良人相信生活本身就是目的——不是为了工作、赚钱或成功，而是为了享受当下。

New Orleans locals know the best po'boys aren't in the French Quarter — they're at neighborhood joints like Parkway Bakery, Domilise's, or Guy's in Uptown, where the bread is fresh, the shrimp is fried to order, and the sandwiches are big enough to share (but you won't).

098

新奥尔良人会告诉你："我们不是美国的一部分，我们是加勒比海的一部分。"

True New Orleanians measure someone's local credentials by how they pronounce street names: Burgundy (bur-GUN-dee, not BUR-gun-dee), Calliope (CAL-ee-ope, not cah-LIE-oh-pee), and Tchoupitoulas (chop-ih-TOO-lis). Getting these wrong marks you as a tourist instantly.

099

新奥尔良是一座活在当下的城市——它可能明天就被淹没，可能永远贫穷，可能永远混乱，但它选择了唱歌、跳舞、喝酒、做爱，把每一天都当作最后一天来过。这种态度可能不负责任，但它让新奥尔良成为地球上最有生命力的地方之一。

New Orleanians have a sacred daily ritual: the porch sit. Evening hours are spent on the front porch, waving at neighbors, nursing a drink, and watching the world go by. It's the original social network.

100

最后，新奥尔良人会用一句话总结这座城市："Laissez les bons temps rouler." 让美好时光滚动起来。

The essence of New Orleans can be captured in a single scene: a second-line parade turning the corner on a Sunday afternoon — a brass band blasting, umbrellas twirling, strangers dancing together, children on shoulders, old folks tapping their feet on porches. No tickets, no permission, no agenda. Just pure, defiant joy in a city that has every reason to weep but chooses, always, to dance.
